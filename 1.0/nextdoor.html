Nextdoor

Engineering & Design Intern · Summer 2015 · San Francisco, CA

The summer before my senior year I spent at Nextdoor, where I worked on both the engineering and design teams on various projects as a KPCB Engineering Fellow. Prior to my summer internship, I also did some part-time work, performing a consistency audit for the company that later transformed into a style guide.

The Challenge

Prior to starting, Nextdoor had a fair amount of consistency issues, particularly on its web platform. As a social network for neighborhoods, issues in design consistency don't fare well with users, as trust on a private social network is paramount. I was tasked with finding the discrepancies among fonts and colors on the website and in the codebase, and resolving them to establish more common styling.

The Approach

Finding slightly off greens and incorrect font sizes involved scouring all of the user flows in the web platform, one by one. While the work seemed slow, it was a great way to become familiar with the features of Nextdoor and recognize room for improvement.

The Solution

Over the course of the summer, in addition to my projects, I spent my free time at work finding and resolving these inconsistencies in the codebase. It gave me a chance to get familiar with the styling in the millions of lines of code, and leveraged my skills as a cross-functional engineer and designer. For Refine Week, an internal event during the summer focused on refining and iterating existing features on the platform, I created a style guide from my consistency audit work that was released to the whole company.
